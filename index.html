Research

Independent Study – Bomyi Lim Lab

During my independent study in Dr. Bomyi Lim’s Lab at the University of Pennsylvania, I investigated the dynamics of molecular crowding and phase separation in protein-RNA systems, with implications for understanding membraneless organelles. I contributed to the experimental design, execution, and analysis of microscopy and spectrophotometric assays to quantify droplet behavior and protein interactions. This research honed my skills in fluorescence microscopy, image analysis, and biophysical characterization methods, and deepened my interest in the intersection of chemical engineering and molecular biology.

HAST Lab – McKay Orthopaedic Research Laboratory

I conducted research in the HAST Lab at the McKay Orthopaedic Research Laboratory under Dr. Michael Hast (currently at the University of Delaware), focusing on bone tissue engineering for critical-sized defect repairs. Our work involved the development and evaluation of 3D-printed polycaprolactone (PCL) scaffolds—known as “Bonuts”—with triply periodic minimal surface architectures designed to improve osteointegration. I co-authored studies that explored the effects of zinc release from GelMA hydrogels on gene expression, metabolic activity, and mechanical properties in vitro. This experience strengthened my technical expertise in scaffold fabrication, hydrogel synthesis, mechanical testing, and confocal imaging, as well as my scientific writing and collaboration skills.
